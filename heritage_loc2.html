DAMBULLA CAVE TEMPLE



This UNESCO World Heritage Site, located 160 meters above Dambulla's central city, has sacred caves with centuries of stories that have resulted in the five pristine Buddhist shrine chambers that travelers can visit today.



A 14-meter rock figure of Lord Buddha may be seen at the Devaraja Lena, also known as The Cave of the Divine King. The neighboring Maha Raja Vihare, also known as the Cave of Great Kings, maintains Buddhist devotion with 56 statues of the Lord Buddha and other deities. The same cave has a giant statue of the ancient King Valagamba, who took refuge in these caves for fourteen years during a South Indian invasion, but after regaining control of his realm, transformed them into the respected shrine we see today.



The Maha Aluth Vihare, or Great New Monastery, has paintings and states from the more recent Kandyan period, while the Paccima Vihare and Devana Aluth Vihare, which have 153 images of the Lord Buddha and over 1,500 ceiling murals, provide a deeper dive into Sri Lanka's Buddhist legacy.
